# BDB Founder Decisions

---

## Decision 001

### Name

BDB OS

### Decision

BDB OS is a connected Business Operating System for SMEs.

It is not simply invoicing software or accounting software.

Every business tool should work under one connected platform.

### Why?

Businesses waste time moving information between disconnected software.

BDB should become the single place from which a business is run.

### Principle

Everything connects.

## Decision 002

### Name

Offline-first business continuity.

### Decision

BDB OS should eventually operate using an offline-first philosophy.

Core business information such as customers, invoices, payments, documents and reports should remain accessible even if the internet connection is lost.

Cloud services, AI, email sending, reminders and synchronisation may require an internet connection, but the business itself should never stop operating because the internet stopped.

### Why?

Businesses should not lose productivity because of internet outages or server downtime.

BDB should continue supporting the business even when external services are unavailable.

### Principle

A business should never stop because the internet stopped.

## Decision 003

### Name

AI assists. Humans decide.

### Decision

Artificial Intelligence should assist users by making recommendations, identifying issues and suggesting solutions.

AI should never silently perform important accounting or financial actions without human approval.

Whenever an important conflict or financial decision occurs, the user remains responsible for approving the final action.

### Why?

Business owners remain legally and financially responsible for their records.

AI should reduce workload, not remove accountability.

### Example

If two invoices are created offline with the same invoice number, BDB should open the Conflict Resolution Centre.

The AI may suggest renumbering one invoice or identifying a duplicate, but a human must approve the final decision.

### Principle

AI assists.
Humans remain accountable.

## Decision 004

### Name

Show information only when it becomes useful.

### Decision

BDB OS should never overwhelm the user with unnecessary information.

The homepage should remain clean, elegant and welcoming.

Detailed reports, financial graphs, cash flow and statistics should only appear when the user enters the relevant workspace or specifically requests them.

### Why?

Most business software overwhelms users by displaying every metric immediately.

The homepage should help users decide where to work, not drown them in information.

### Principle

Show information when it becomes useful, not before.

---

## Decision 005

### Name

The software adapts to the user.

### Decision

Every user should be able to customise their own workspace.

Frequently used modules can be pinned for quick access.

BDB may observe usage patterns and suggest improvements, but it should never force changes on the user.

### Why?

A finance employee, warehouse manager and managing director all use different parts of the system.

Their experience should reflect the way they work.

### Principle

The software adapts to the user.
The user should never have to adapt to the software.

---

## Decision 006

### Name

Software should feel calm.

### Decision

The emotional experience of using BDB OS is as important as its functionality.

Animations should be subtle.

The interface should feel modern, elegant and spacious.

White space should be embraced.

Information should have room to breathe.

The software should never make users feel stressed or overwhelmed.

### Why?

Business owners already deal with enough pressure.

Their software should reduce stress, improve focus and inspire confidence.

### Principle

Calm software creates confident users.
